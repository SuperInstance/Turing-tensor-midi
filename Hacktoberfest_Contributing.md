# 🎃 Contributing to Turing-An_AI_Integrated_Real-time_Assistant for Hacktoberfest 2025

First off, thank you for considering contributing to **Turing**! Hacktoberfest is a fantastic time to get involved in open source, and we're excited to have you here.

We are actively participating in **Hacktoberfest 2025**. A Pull Request (PR) made in this repository during the month of October will count toward your Hacktoberfest completion,
provided it meets both our guidelines and the official Hacktoberfest quality standards.

---

## ⭐️ Official Hacktoberfest Rules & Quality Standards

Your contributions must adhere to the official [Hacktoberfest rules](https://hacktoberfest.com/participation/#:~:text=Rules) to be counted:

1.  **Register:** You must register on the [Hacktoberfest official website](https://hacktoberfest.com/) to participate.
2.  **Timeframe:** Pull requests must be submitted between **October 1 and October 31**.
3.  **Quality:** **All PRs must be meaningful and of high quality.** Low-quality contributions will be marked as spam and may disqualify you from Hacktoberfest.
    * **Examples of low-quality contributions (that may be marked as spam):**
        * Automated PRs (e.g., script-generated).
        * PRs that simply add whitespace, fix typos in insignificant places, or perform minor cleanups that don't add value.
        * PRs that are clearly an attempt to simply *+1* your contribution count.

---

## 🔎 How to Find an Issue

We use the following labels to help you find suitable issues:

* **`hacktoberfest`**: Use this label to filter for issues that are explicitly open and encouraged for Hacktoberfest contributions.
* **`good first issue`**: Perfect for new contributors to open source or to this project. These are typically smaller, well-defined tasks.
* **`help wanted`**: For more complex issues where we specifically need community assistance.

**To get started:**

1.  Go to the **[Issues page]**(https://github.com/Shashank-Tripathi-07/Turing-An_AI_Integrated_Real-time_Assistant/issues).
2.  Filter issues by the **`hacktoberfest`** label or start a new issue if you find a new issue.
3.  Comment on the issue you'd like to work on to let others know you've claimed it.
